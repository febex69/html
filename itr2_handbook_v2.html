01 //Core Mechanics
What Is ITR2?

An immersive VR survival shooter set in the Pechorsk Anomaly Zone — a post-apocalyptic exclusion zone inspired by the novel Roadside Picnic. Survive hostile entities, deadly anomalies, and the ever-approaching Tide. Solo or 2-player co-op (4-player at full release).

VR Requirements

Requires OpenXR. Supported on PC VR and Meta Quest. HTC Vive Wands and Windows Mixed Reality (WMR) are NOT supported. Uses 6DoF controllers for full physical interaction.

Security Levels

Progress through Security Levels 0–3 (and beyond). Higher levels unlock better weapons, gear, and zones at the Exchange. Earn SC (Supply Credits) to buy unlocked items.

Modular Weapons

Patch 0.14: 30 gun frames + 156 modules. Swap barrels, handguards, dust covers, pistol grips, buttstocks. Each part affects weight, accuracy, reliability, and recoil. Mix and match freely.

01.1 //Essential Controls

Weapon Handling

Use guns as you would in real life — safety on, finger off trigger until ready. Reload by physically swapping magazines. Press-check your magazine to see remaining rounds. You can hold two mags in one hand.

Knife Combat

Destroy Fragment orbs after shooting them down. Throw knives at Spawns to save ammo. Strafe to dodge Spawn lunges, then knife them. Knives are free to use and silent.

Anomaly Probes

Throw probes ahead of you to trigger and detect anomalies. There is ALWAYS a safe path through any anomaly field. Probes light up when hitting active anomalies — plan your corridor carefully.

Artifact Detector (AD-15)

Hold the detector and pull the trigger to send a light flash — this spawns nearby artifacts. Beeping speed increases as you get closer. Color shifts from blue to green when you face the artifact. Always carry it on your shoulder.

Gas Mask

Required for gas anomaly zones and the Distortion Zone. Filters deplete over time. Watch the timer. Carry at least 3 spare filters when going deep. No mask = very fast death in gas zones.

Map & Navigation

Your physical map is your lifeline. Mark anomaly fields where you've found artifacts before — they always respawn after each Tide. Also check the Tide timer regularly so you're never caught outside.

Inventory & Weight

Place pouches, holsters, and pouches on your chest rig, armor vest, and backpack at any angle. Weight matters — overloaded explorers move slower. Know your carry limit.

MAINTENANCE: Guns degrade with every shot. Clean and repair at base. A jammed gun mid-firefight is frequently fatal. High reliability stat = less chance of misfire.

03.1 //Ammo Type Breakdown
FMJ — Full Metal Jacket

Standard round. Balanced damage and cost. Default choice for unarmored enemies. Available for every caliber.

P+ — Overpressure

+50% damage and armor penetration. Significantly more expensive. Worth it for tough fights. Available for 9×18mm caliber weapons.

AP — Armor Piercing

MANDATORY vs armored Mimics. Standard ammo does essentially no damage to armored enemies. Always carry AP when going into high-threat zones.

SS — Subsonic

Reduced damage and armor pen. Much quieter — ideal with a suppressor for stealth. Less expensive than standard FMJ.

TR — Tracer

Shows bullet trajectory. Useful for learning where your shots land. Slight penalty vs standard FMJ but helps with accuracy training.

Slug

Shotgun single solid slug. Excellent penetration and damage at distance. Use vs armored targets where buckshot fails.

04 //Attachments & Modules

Patch 0.14.0 introduced 156 modular components across 30 gun frames. Parts slot into specific positions on each gun frame.

Barrels

Fit in front of the receiver. Affect weight, muzzle velocity, accuracy, and loudness. Guns with swappable barrels cannot fire without one installed. Short barrels = lighter, longer barrels = more accuracy and velocity.

Handguards

Fit over the barrel. Affect weight, reliability, and provide additional attachment slots. For most guns you cannot fire two-handed without a handguard (except the Kedr).

Pistol Grips

Required to hold the weapon and pull the trigger. Enable Picatinny rail attachments. Enable special scopes on some weapons. Cannot fire without one.

Buttstocks

Affect recoil, weight, and comfort. Folding stocks allow the gun to be stored more compactly. Some allow for AR-style stocks to be fitted on SMGs via adapters.

Dust Covers

Sit on top of the receiver. Railed versions (Railed Dust Cover) add a Picatinny top rail, enabling red dot sights and scopes that wouldn't otherwise fit.

Suppressors

Dramatically reduce noise, preventing enemy alerts. Most SMGs and pistols support them. Some rifles need a barrel adapter. NOT available for most shotguns. Huge stealth advantage — priority purchase.

PRIORITY: Get a suppressor for your main weapon as soon as possible. Silent kills don't alert nearby enemies. The difference between stealthy zone-clearing and a three-Mimic ambush.

05 //Hostile Entities

The Pechorsk Zone hosts multiple types of hostile entities. Each requires a different approach. Never fight on their terms — always control the engagement.

☗
Fragment
CommonMelee

Hostile humanoid entities appearing as fragmented floating statue chunks. Hard to spot in anomaly fields — their ash-coloured body parts blend with the environment. Makes a distinctive clicking sound while walking and screams when it aggros. Will close distance rapidly.

After enough shots, collapses and becomes a floating black orb. The orb MUST be destroyed or the Fragment will reform. Shoot the orb or stab it with a knife.

// KILL: Shoot until it drops → destroy the floating black orb with a knife or extra shots. Knife is faster and saves ammo. Retreat and reload if overwhelmed — you outrun them at sprint speed.
⚠
Spawn
CommonFast

Small, fast creatures that attack in groups. Multiple variants exist. They move directly at you in a straight line then launch a jumping lunge attack. Individually easy but dangerous in large groups. They are immune to anomalies, so they can pursue you through hazard zones.

Most pistols will kill them in one or two shots. Knife kills are recommended to save ammo — strafe to bait their lunge, then strike while they're recovering.

// KILL: Strafe sideways as Spawn approaches → it lunges and misses → stab or shoot while grounded. Shotguns are devastating against groups. Never stand still.
⧬
Mimic
CommonRanged

Humanoid entities resembling former zone residents with warped, distorted appearances. Armed with various real firearms. Use cover, suppress your position, and peek around corners to shoot you.

Variants: Militsioner (PM pistol, 55hp), Jaeger (IZh-27 shotgun), and others. Ambush Mimics spawn silently at specific points. All Mimics occasionally emit eerie dialogue. Armored variants require AP ammo.

// KILL: Use cover. Flank when they're reloading. Never stand in the open. Armored Mimics: AP/P+ ammo mandatory — standard rounds barely scratch armor. Use grenades on clusters.
◈
Heavy Mimic (Gunner)
RareElite

The most dangerous entity in the zone. Wields an RPK-74 machine gun with a 75-round drum — it can sustain fire for a very long time. Found rarely in the Forest and later zones.

Covered in full body armor: armored vest, shoulder pads, and a Maska-1Sch bulletproof helmet with MVD urban camouflage. Very high health pool. Will fire in controlled bursts and suppress you behind cover.

// KILL: AP ammo only. Approach from flank or rear. Grenades are highly effective. NEVER fight it in the open. If you hear the RPK, take cover immediately and plan your approach.
⬟
Armored Mimic Variants
Mid-LateRanged

Upgraded versions of regular Mimics with body armor added to their distorted forms. Found in later zones and spawned from Rift anomalies in mid-game. Behave the same as regular Mimics but absorb significantly more damage from standard rounds.

They are the primary reason to always carry AP ammo when heading into the Forest or Outskirts.

// KILL: AP or slug ammo only. Standard FMJ rounds have low penetration chance vs armor. Flanking still works. Aim for exposed head if you can. Avoid open engagements.
☁
Rift Spawned Enemies
Contextual

Black rift anomalies (black mess anomalies) do not spawn enemies naturally — but destroying them (shooting or stabbing) triggers an enemy spawn. They appear with a thunderous crack and flash. Early game: regular Mimics. Late game: armored Mimics.

Destroying rifts also drops valuable Shard artifacts, so they're worth engaging — but be ready for the fight that follows.

// STRATEGY: Clear any nearby enemies before destroying the rift. Destroy rift → immediately take cover and reload → engage spawning Mimics from a defensive position. Have AP ammo ready.
DISTORTION ZONE MIMICS: Only Mimics spawn inside the Distortion Zone (at fixed positions). No Fragments or Spawns. They know the terrain — clear buildings carefully, room by room.
06 //Anomalies

Distorted hazards found throughout the zone. Each has a unique look and behavior. Always use probes before entering any dense anomaly field. There is ALWAYS a safe path.

~

Distortion

Glows pale blue. Deals constant tick damage while you're inside. Very visible — easy to spot and avoid. Probe to map the safe corridor. Most common anomaly type in early zones.

// Navigate around by probing edges. Never try to run through — tick damage stacks fast.
↑

Reflector

Launches thrown objects and you away with great force. Getting hit throws you into other anomalies or off ledges. Be careful when throwing probes — step sideways before impact if one lands near a Reflector.

// Always strafe sideways before probing. Keep distance. Never probe directly ahead without sidestepping first.
☁

Gas

Visible as green-tinted fog clouds and mist. Lethal without a gas mask. Mask filter depletes over time (watch the counter). Artifacts frequently spawn inside gas fields so you'll need to navigate them often.

// Gas mask on before entering ANY green-tinted zone. Carry 3+ spare filters. Replace before the timer hits 0.
⚡

Shock / Lightning

Electrical discharge anomaly. Deals heavy burst damage. Very difficult to navigate. If you die inside a shock field, retrieving your gear is extremely hard. Anomalies reroll after each Tide — if stuck, wait.

// Never die inside shock fields. Back off and wait for Tide reroll if you can't find a path.
■

Rift (Black Mess)

Dark buzzing blob anomaly. Shoot or stab to destroy — drops a Shard artifact. WARNING: destroying a Rift spawns Mimics (armored in later zones) with a huge thunderclap. Rifts also respawn enemies periodically if left alive.

// Clear nearby enemies first. Destroy rift → take cover immediately → engage spawned Mimics. Very profitable if done right.
▓

Stomper

INVISIBLE. Detected only by loud stomping sounds and dust/ground disturbances. Knock you down and deal heavy damage. CRITICAL: do NOT carry explosives near Stompers — they can detonate your grenades. Found in Pobeda Factory and later areas.

// Stop and listen. Move around stomping sounds carefully. Drop grenades before entering known Stomper areas.
◇

Distortion Zone Fog

An entire area blanketed in red fog with yellow floating particles. Massive continuous tick damage without the special protection mask. The mask gives 150 seconds of protection per use. Very high relic density inside — frequent visit destination in mid/late game.

// Special mask required, not gas mask. Time your runs. Farm relics between mask uses. Mimics inside at fixed positions.
⬡

The Cube

Appears only in the Outskirts area. A massive cube made of spheres slides slowly across the sky with three orbiting spheres rotating around it. An environmental anomaly — primarily a surreal visual hazard. Stay out of its path.

// Watch the sky in Outskirts. The Cube's path is predictable — observe before crossing open areas.
◯

Altered Water

Blood-red water found around the Peninsula. Lethal to enter. The narrow land of the Peninsula borders directly onto Altered Water — one misstep off the edge is death. Fog on the Peninsula reduces visibility significantly.

// On the Peninsula, always know where the edges are. Move carefully in fog. Never sprint near the edges.
UNIVERSAL PROBE RULE: Throw probes aggressively ahead of you in clusters. There is ALWAYS a safe corridor through any anomaly field — the game guarantees it. Probe the edges first, then walk slowly. Never brute-force through anomalies.
07 //Artifacts

Valuable items found inside anomaly fields using the AD-15 Detector. The primary source of SC income. Artifact locations reset each Tide but fields that have spawned artifacts will always keep spawning them.

Finding Artifacts

1. Hold the AD-15 Detector. Pull the trigger to send a flash — this spawns nearby artifacts.
2. Detector beeps faster as you get closer.
3. Color changes from blue → green when you face the artifact.
4. Navigate through the anomaly field with probes.
5. The artifact is always in a reachable safe area.

Artifact Hunting Tips

• Keep your detector on your shoulder at all times — it beeps passively while you explore.
• Anomaly fields respawn artifacts every Tide — memorize productive fields.
• Bronze-colored artifacts are most common and primarily sell items.
• Community maps show all known artifact spawn locations.
• Clear enemies BEFORE hunting in a field — enemies ignore anomalies.

08 //Zone Locations

The Pechorsk Exclusion Zone has multiple explorable areas, each with distinct terrain, threats, and loot profiles.

Vanno Base
Safe Zone

UNPSC headquarters. Your home. Access the Exchange (buy/sell), maintain weapons, sleep to skip time and trigger the Tide safely. All storage here is 100% safe. Always return before Tide hits.

TIP: The firing range is free — try guns before buying.
Peninsula
Starting Area / Sec 0-1

First area explored. Land narrows dangerously — Altered Water on all sides. Dense fog reduces visibility. High relic concentration despite being early-game. Watch your footing near edges. Good early SC farming due to relic density.

DANGER: Fog + narrow land = easy death by fall. Move carefully.
Forest
Mid-Game / Sec 1-2

Large area combining forest and industrial elements. A major river splits it — two bridges (north and south) are the only crossings. Takes significant travel time. Heavy Mimics spawn here. Multiple artifact fields. Key mid-game farming zone.

Pechorsk Outskirts
Late-Game / Sec 2-3

Large urban area added in 2025 update. Dense building interiors, streets, industrial zones. The Cube anomaly only appears here. Excellent high-value loot. Creates many Mimic ambush opportunities — clear methodically. New with patch 0.14.

Distortion Zone
Late-Game / Sec 3

Red fog everywhere, yellow floating particles. Requires special protection mask (not gas mask) — 150s per use. Only Mimics spawn here. Extremely high relic density. The best late-game farming area once you have the mask.

WRONG MASK: Gas mask does NOT work here. You need the Distortion Zone mask.
Pobeda Factory
Mid-Game

Industrial factory complex. Dense with Mimics and tight corridors. Suppressors are essential here — gunshots echo and attract everything. Stomper anomalies present. Multiple floors and hidden stash locations including the IZh-81 shotgun stash.

Hideouts
Field Stash

Small safe shelters scattered throughout zones. Items stored here SURVIVE the Tide (unlike regular ground loot). Critical for caching overflow weapons and loot during long runs. You can rest here. Not as safe as Vanno for you personally.

EXPLOIT: When your bag is full and Tide is near, cache overflow in a hideout rather than abandoning it.
Pechorsk Castle
Late-Game

Final story location. Accessed through Kolkhoz Zarya. Bring maximum health, AP ammo, body armor, gas mask, and probes. Dense anomalies and armored Mimics throughout. The Grail artifact is found here on a pedestal at the top.

PREP: Never enter underprepared. This is the hardest area in the game.
09 //The Tide

The Tide is a periodic reset event that sweeps through the entire Pechorsk Zone. Understanding it is essential for efficient play.

What the Tide Does

• Resets ALL enemies (respawns and repositions them)
• Rerolls all loot locations
• Resets artifact positions in known fields
• Slightly repositions anomaly clusters (fixed ones stay, random ones reroll)
• Kills you if you're outside when it fully hits
• Occurs every 4–5 in-game days (adjustable in settings)

What SURVIVES the Tide

• Everything in your Vanno base storage
• Everything in hideout stashes
• Your character and inventory
• The locations of all anomaly fields (they always respawn)
• Your money (SC) and security level
• Any items you're physically carrying

09.1 //Warning System
24h
FIRST WARNING

Loud audio alert. Begin planning your route back. Can still complete most missions at this stage.

12h
SECOND WARNING

Louder than gunshots. Drop what you're doing and start heading home unless very close to completing a mission.

0
TIDE HITS

Zone goes grey. If outside: teleported to a random zone location surrounded by enemies. Effectively a death sentence.

TIDE EXPLOITATION: The Tide rerolls anomaly positions. If you have a field with unreachable artifacts (bad shock anomaly spawn), sleep to the next Tide to get a fresh layout.
BROKEN SHARD: This mission artifact disappears the moment the Tide hits. If you pick one up, get back to Vanno immediately — no exceptions.
HIDEOUT HACK: When your bag is full and Tide is close, stash overflow items in a hideout. They survive. Come back after the Tide for them.
10 //Economy & Exchange

Supply Credits (SC) are earned by selling items and completing missions. Manage your SC carefully — good gear is expensive and essential for survival.

Best SC Sources

1. Story missions (highest payout)
2. Artifact hunting — bronze artifacts in known fields
3. Shard artifacts (from Rift anomalies)
4. Scientific equipment (weather monitors ~200 SC, photo traps ~300 SC)
5. Documents (always pick up, try selling)
6. Anomalous items
7. Enemy weapon looting

Weapon Looting Tips

Enemy guns can be looted and sold. Early game: one gun on back, one in bag. Later: use hideout stash for overflow. Return for cached weapons after the Tide. Guns that can't fit can be temporarily hidden in nearby buildings.

Smart Spending

Priority buys: suppressor → body armor → better primary weapon → helmet. Try weapons at the firing range before buying. Adjust difficulty value modifier to increase sell prices if struggling. Ammo cost is the biggest recurring expense.

11 //Gear & Equipment

Non-weapon gear that every explorer needs. Prioritize based on what zones you're entering.

Body Armor

Bullet protection. Adds weight. Essential in mid-to-late game when Mimics start shooting. The extra weight is worth the protection. Cannot be skipped once you're hitting the Factory and Forest areas.

Helmet

Head protection against bullets. ~1200 SC. Adds weight. Highly recommended once Gunner Mimics appear. A headshot from an RPK-74 without a helmet is instantly fatal.

Gas Mask

Required for gas anomaly zones. Filters deplete over time — watch the timer. Carry 3+ spare filters when planning deep zone runs. NOT compatible with Distortion Zone.

Distortion Zone Mask

Special mask for entering the red-fog Distortion Zone. Provides 150 seconds of protection per use. NOT a gas mask — a completely different item. Essential for late-game relic farming.

Anomaly Probes

Small throwable devices that trigger anomalies on impact. Your most important exploration tool. Carry large quantities. Advanced Probes have cloth strips for different flight behavior (cosmetic only).

AD-15 Artifact Detector

Tool for finding artifacts. Beeps faster near artifacts, turns green when aimed at them. Pull trigger to send a flash that spawns nearby artifacts. Wear it on your shoulder at all times for passive detection.

Health Syringes

Primary healing item. Use physically — jab into your arm. Carry several. Don't wait until low HP to use — use them as soon as you take significant damage so you survive the next hit.

Chest Rig System

The loadout customization system. Attach pouches, mag holders, and holsters to your chest rig, body armor, and backpack at any position and angle. Optimize your layout for fast VR access. Magazine placement is critical — reach matters in firefights.

Grenades

RDG-1 fragmentation grenade. Devastating against groups of enemies and Heavy Mimics. Do NOT carry near Stompers — they can trigger detonation. Use from behind cover. Aim at corners to flush out Mimics.

LOADOUT SETUP: In VR, where you place your mags matters as much as which guns you carry. Put your primary weapon's mags where your off-hand naturally reaches. Practice your reload before heading into the zone.
12 //Survival Tips
Combat

Magazine Check

After every fight, reload. Don't wait until the next fight starts to find out you have 2 rounds left. The PM especially — 8 rounds goes fast.

Suppressor First

Buying a suppressor should be your first major purchase. Silent kills don't alert nearby enemies. In the Factory, this is the difference between one fight and five.

Retreat is Valid

You outrun Fragments at sprint speed. Fall back, reload, recompose. Never panic and waste ammo. Come back with a plan.

AP Ammo Always

Carry AP rounds when heading into Forest and beyond. Armored Mimics are immune to standard FMJ. Shotgun slugs also work on armored targets at close range.

Dead Explorer Loot

Bodies scattered in the zone often have ammo. Not always much, but worth checking when passing by — a few extra rounds matters.

Exploration

Night vs Day

Scavenge during the day. Night allows stealthier movement but harder to spot targets. Use your flashlight or torch when needed.

Mark Your Fields

Mentally (or physically) note every anomaly field where you've found an artifact. They ALWAYS spawn again after the Tide. Those are your money fields.

Spare Filters

Never enter a gas zone with one filter. Carry at least 3. Never enter the Distortion Zone without timing your runs to your mask's 150-second timer.

Weight Management

Late-game loot piles up fast. Know your carry limit. Use hideouts mid-run. Abandoned loot is lost SC.

Firing Range is Free

Test every weapon in the firing range before spending SC. Recoil, handling, and iron sights feel very different in VR than on paper.
